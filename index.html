The unmodified H-a:Si thin-film cell. Reflects ~30% of incident light at normal incidence. Performance drops sharply at oblique angles. This is the direct baseline the KIT nanostructure enhancement figures are measured against.

A single-layer thin film tuned for a specific wavelength reduces reflection on the same H-a:Si absorber. Improves on the flat baseline but degrades at high incidence angles and works best over a narrow spectral range.

Disordered nanoholes of varying diameters (133–343 nm) trap and scatter light across the full spectrum. Absorption rises continuously with angle — reaching +207% at 50°. Works with any thin-film PV technology at industrial scale.

05 — Real-World Impact

Where This Technology
Changes Everything

Because these nanostructures can be applied to any thin-film PV technology at industrial scale, the applications span from rooftops to wearables.

🏠
Rooftop Solar
Fixed panels no longer need to track the sun. Omnidirectional absorption maximises yield through dawn, dusk and overcast days.
🌤
Diffuse Light Climates
Particularly valuable in high-latitude regions with frequent cloud cover where direct perpendicular light is rare.
⌚
Wearables & IoT
Thin-film cells on curved, flexible surfaces benefit from angle-independence, powering smartwatches and sensors without fixed orientation.
🏙
Building-Integrated PV
Vertical facades and irregular architectural surfaces previously unsuitable for solar can now generate meaningful power.
